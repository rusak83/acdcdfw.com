Common Problems

Viking Cooktop Problems We Fix

🔥 Burner Not Igniting (Gas)

Clogged burner ports, cracked igniter electrode, failed spark module. Victor isolates the exact component.

🔁 Continuous Clicking

Moisture in spark switch after a spill, or failed igniter module. On gas cooktops, a stuck igniter is a safety concern.

🌊 Weak or Uneven Flame

Partially blocked burner ports or low gas pressure. Victor cleans ports and checks gas supply on-site.

⚡ Electric Element Not Heating

Burned-out burner element, switch failure, or control board issue. Specific element replaced without disrupting the others.

🔲 Induction Zone Not Working

Induction coil failure, control board issue, or flex connector problem. Victor tests each zone independently.

🎛️ Touch Controls Not Responding

Control board failure or touch panel issue. Common on older Viking electric and induction cooktops.

Viking Cooktop Repair FAQ

Three most common causes: (1) clogged burner ports from food spills — ports need cleaning; (2) cracked or dirty igniter electrode — won't generate spark; (3) failed spark module — controls all igniter electrodes. Victor tests each component to find the exact failure.
