Why KitchenAid Repairs Need the Right Diagnosis

KitchenAid uses premium configurations across refrigeration, dishwashing, cooking, and laundry, but most failures still come down to specific repairable components.

⭐

French Door Refrigeration

KitchenAid refrigerators commonly fail at evaporator fans, defrost systems, dispenser circuits, and ice makers. Similar symptoms can come from different parts, so the cooling system has to be tested before parts are ordered.

🧊

Dishwasher Wash-System Failures

KitchenAid dishwashers often fail at the wash motor, drain pump, sump, diverter, or latch assembly. Cleaning complaints and draining complaints can come from different parts of the same system.

🔌

Oven and Range Electronics

KitchenAid ovens and ranges commonly fail at bake elements, igniters, temperature sensors, and control boards. A no-heat complaint still needs full circuit testing before any board is condemned.

🛡️

Laundry Platform Failures

KitchenAid washers and dryers commonly fail at drain pumps, door locks, suspension parts, heating elements, rollers, and moisture sensors. These are usually repairable issues when diagnosed early.

Common Problems

KitchenAid Issues We Fix Daily

Range Burner Not Lighting

Ignition failures usually come from a bad spark switch, electrode, burner head, or control issue. We test the full burner circuit before replacing parts.

Oven Not Reaching Temperature

Bake igniter (gas) or bake element (electric) failure. Also: convection fan motor failure causes hot spots. KitchenAid Professional ovens have a dual convection system — one fan failure throws off baking significantly.

Refrigerator Not Cooling

Condenser coils, evaporator fan motors, thermistors, defrost parts, or sealed-system problems can all cause the same symptom. Refrigerator diagnosis has to separate airflow faults from true cooling-system failures.

Dishwasher Not Draining

Standing water can come from a blocked drain path, failed drain pump, sump issue, or control fault. We trace the full drain circuit instead of guessing at one part.

Continuous Clicking (Burner)

Continuous clicking usually means moisture intrusion, a stuck switch, or a failing ignition component. We isolate the bad part and restore normal burner operation.

Washer Not Spinning

Spin failures can come from drain issues, lid or door locks, suspension wear, drive components, or control problems. We test the full spin sequence before quoting the repair.
