Common Problems

Thermador Microwave Problems We Fix

Thermador microwaves combine tight built-in installation with high-voltage components. Victor diagnoses the failed part, whether that is the door circuit, heating circuit, or control board.

🍽️ Runs But Does Not Heat

Classic magnetron, diode, or capacitor failure. If the timer runs and the light turns on but food stays cold, the high-voltage heating circuit needs service.

⚡ Sparking or Arcing

Burned waveguide cover, damaged cavity coating, or metal rack contact. Arcing should be addressed immediately before it damages the cavity or magnetron.

🚪 Door Won't Latch or Start

Door switch and latch bracket failures are common on built-in units. If the microwave thinks the door is open, it will not start the cook cycle.

🔢 Display or Keypad Not Responding

Dead buttons, partial keypad response, or blank display usually point to the user interface board or ribbon connection problem.

🔁 Turntable Not Turning

Turntable motor, coupler, or control relay failure. Uneven heating often starts here, especially on older built-in Thermador microwaves.

🧩 Combo Unit Microwave Faults

Microwave/oven combination units can fail in the microwave section while the lower oven still works. We isolate the microwave subsystem instead of condemning the whole appliance.
