Common Problems

Thermador Washer Problems We Fix

Thermador front-load washers are reliable — but drain systems, door locks, and bearings wear over time. Victor diagnoses the exact component, not the whole machine.

🚫 Not Draining (E18)

Error code E18 = drain fault. Most common cause: clogged drain filter (behind the front access panel — should be cleaned every 3 months). Also check kinked drain hose and blocked standpipe. Victor resolves most E18 errors on first visit.

🔄 Not Spinning

Washer fills and washes but won't spin out water. Causes: failed motor control board, worn carbon brushes on motor, or drum bearing failure. Victor diagnoses which component is failing before ordering parts.

🔒 Door Won't Open

Front-load door locks for safety during cycles. If it won't open after the cycle ends: water still inside (drain issue), door lock interlock failure, or control board not sending unlock signal. Victor pinpoints the cause.

📳 Vibrating Excessively

Thermador washers are engineered for quiet operation. Excessive vibration means: shipping bolts still in place (new units), washer not level, worn shock absorbers, or out-of-balance drum. Victor checks all four causes.

⚠️ Error Code E23

E23 = drain pump fault — the pump itself has failed, not just a clog. The drain pump needs replacement. Victor carries common pump parts for Thermador/Bosch platforms for same-day repair in most cases.

💧 Water Leaking

Front-load leaks most often come from: worn door boot gasket, loose hose connections, or clogged detergent drawer causing overflow. Victor identifies the leak source precisely — no guessing.

Thermador Washer Repair FAQ

E18 means the washer cannot drain within the expected time. The most common cause is a clogged drain filter — located behind a small access panel at the front bottom of the machine. It should be cleaned every 3 months. Other causes: kinked drain hose, blocked standpipe, or failed drain pump. Victor diagnoses and resolves E18 on the first visit in most cases.

E23 is a drain pump fault — the pump motor itself has failed, not just a clog. E18 and E23 are related but distinct: E18 is a drainage timing failure (often a clog), E23 is a pump hardware failure. E23 requires drain pump replacement. Victor carries Thermador/Bosch drain pumps on the truck for same-day repair in most cases.
