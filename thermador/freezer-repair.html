Common Problems

Thermador Freezer Issues We Fix

Thermador freezer failures follow predictable patterns. Here's what we see most often across DFW.

Not Freezing

Failed evaporator fan motor (most common), compressor failure, refrigerant leak, or a control board not calling for cooling. We diagnose the specific failed component — not guess. Temperature tests and component checks done on-site.

Excessive Frost Buildup

Defrost system failure — the heater, defrost thermostat, or defrost control — causes ice to accumulate on the evaporator coil. Eventually blocks airflow entirely. We replace the faulty component, manually defrost the coil, and verify the defrost cycle timing.

Door Seal Failure

A cracked or compressed door gasket lets warm air in continuously. Signs: frost around the door frame, compressor running non-stop, temperature that won't hold. We replace Thermador freezer door seals and test the seal with a dollar bill test post-install.

Temperature Fluctuations

Temperature cycling above 0°F signals a problem with the thermostat, temperature sensor, or defrost system. On column models paired with a refrigerator column, we check whether the shared control board is affecting both units.

Loud Noises

Buzzing, rattling, or clicking — compressor start relay failure, failing evaporator fan bearing, or ice hitting the fan blades. We identify the noise source and replace the worn component before it leads to complete failure.

Error Codes

Thermador freezers display E-codes similar to their refrigerator counterparts. E20 = evaporator sensor, E30 = defrost heater, E50 = compressor fault. We read and interpret the full error sequence to identify the root cause — not just clear the code.
